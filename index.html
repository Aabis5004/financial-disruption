The Current System is Outdated

Take a simple example: buying tickets at a stadium with your credit card seems easy on the surface, right? But behind the scenes there is an entire stack of intermediaries involved just to make that one transaction work:

  • Point of sale (POS) system
  • Payment processor
  • Issuing bank
  • Acquiring bank and other service providers

Each of these players takes a cut from the transaction - that could be multiple percentage points in fees just to let you buy a ticket.

International Transactions Are Even Worse

When you try to send or receive money across borders, that entire stack gets duplicated on the other side. There is a payment processor in one country, then another one in the destination country, same for the banks and gateways - all of them take their own cut, all of them add friction.
